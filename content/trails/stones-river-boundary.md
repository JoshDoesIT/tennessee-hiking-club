The Boundary Trail loops around the edge of the park through second-growth cedar and hardwood, connecting the battlefield's main features without the car traffic of the tour road. It passes the Hazen Brigade Monument, raised by soldiers in 1863 and thought to be the oldest intact Civil War monument, along with the National Cemetery and the rocky cedar glades that shaped the fighting here in the winter of 1862 to 1863. The grade is nearly flat, making the full loop a long but easy walk.
