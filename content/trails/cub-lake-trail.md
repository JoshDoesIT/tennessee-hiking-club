Cub Lake is the day-use heart of Natchez Trace State Park, and this loop stays close to the water the whole way around, crossing the lake's arms on two wooden footbridges and passing the swim beach, fishing pier, and cabins before returning through oak, hickory, and planted loblolly pine. The grades are mild and the footing is mostly packed dirt, but roots and short muddy stretches near the shoreline linger after rain, and the bridges can be slick on frosty mornings. Summer brings heavy West Tennessee humidity, mosquitoes, and the occasional copperhead sunning at the trail edge, so long pants and bug spray earn their keep from May through September. The park is free to enter and allows leashed dogs; the connector from the campground joins the loop on the south shore if you are staying overnight.
