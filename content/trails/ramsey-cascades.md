Ramsey Cascades is the tallest waterfall in Great Smoky Mountains National Park, dropping roughly 100 feet over a series of rock ledges. Getting there is the hard part: the trail climbs about 2,200 feet over four miles, the last mile and a half rough and rocky as it follows the Ramsey Prong up through one of the finest stands of old-growth hardwood forest in the East. Giant tulip poplars, silverbells, and basswoods tower over the path. The rocks at the base are slick and have caused fatal falls; enjoy the cascade from a safe distance and do not climb. A parking tag is required park-wide.
