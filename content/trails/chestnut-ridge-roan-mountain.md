The trail begins at the Miller Farmstead, a 1900s Appalachian homestead on the National Register of Historic Places, and climbs through deciduous forest and rhododendron along Chestnut Ridge. It rolls over a series of steep pitches before reaching an overlook platform that frames the Roan Highlands from Big Hump Mountain to Carvers Gap. This is the lower state-park trail, distinct from the high Roan balds.
