This is the signature walk at Fort Pillow, following the yellow-blazed Historical Trail from the museum across a boardwalk over a steep wooded ravine, then up flights of wooden stairs along the Civil War breastworks to the restored inner fort, where reconstructed palisade walls and cannon overlook Cold Creek and the Mississippi River floodplain from the First Chickasaw Bluff. The loop around the fortification adds context from interpretive panels about April 12, 1864, when Confederate troops overran the fort and massacred surrendering Union soldiers, many of them Black artillerymen of the U.S. Colored Troops; the site rewards a slow, thoughtful visit. The bluff edges are abrupt and unfenced in places, the stairs and boardwalk get slick with leaf litter and rain, and summer brings serious mosquitoes, ticks, and chiggers off the river bottoms. Start at the museum to pick up a trail map; the visitor center keeps shorter hours than the park itself.
