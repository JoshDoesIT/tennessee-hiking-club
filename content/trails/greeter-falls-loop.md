Greeter Falls delivers a remarkable amount of drama for under two miles of walking: Firescald Creek pours over a 15-foot upper ledge, slides across a rock shelf, and then plunges 50 feet into one of the prettiest blue-green pools on the plateau. From the trailhead the Greeter Trail runs an easy half mile through hardwoods to the loop split; go right for the upper falls first, then double back for the lower falls spur, where the route descends a slot in the bluff on a steel spiral staircase bolted to the cliff — sturdy, but wet, steep, and not for anyone carrying a squirming dog or toddler. The final approach to the pool crosses jumbled, slick boulders, and the current below the falls is stronger than it looks. The adjacent Blue Hole Trail adds a short side trip to a popular swimming hole on the same creek. This is now part of Savage Gulf State Park, split from South Cumberland in 2022, and remains free day-use.
