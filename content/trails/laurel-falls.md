Laurel Falls is the most popular waterfall hike in the park, and for good reason: a paved path leads 1.3 miles to an 80-foot waterfall that drops in two tiers, upper and lower, with a stone walkway crossing the stream between them. "Paved" does not mean flat or stroller-friendly, though; the trail is uneven, climbs steadily, and has drop-offs without railings, so keep children close. The name comes from the mountain laurel that blooms pink and white along the route in May. Do not climb on the wet rocks at the falls, where people are injured every year. A parking tag is required park-wide.
